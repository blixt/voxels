# Art Direction Bible

## Pillars

- Alien, mineral, weathered, and organic before bright or toy-like.
- Routes should be legible through repeated land language: slabs, cairns, lanterns, ribs, obelisks, shrine fragments, cave-mouth tells, and vegetation changes.
- Props should read as part of a culture or ecosystem, not as generic decoration.
- Atmosphere should vary by region: ash, salt haze, wetland mist, glass glare, fungal glow, cold coast air, and cave darkness.
- UI should be quiet and diegetic where possible. Player-facing labels should be names or natural descriptions, not raw ids.

## Region Cues

- Red Mountain: dark ash, broken silhouettes, harsh visibility, sharp ridges, scorched route markers.
- Wetlands: low islands, blackwater pools, reeds, rib remains, fungal bridges, and muted reflections.
- Glass coast: pale shards, cold light, exposed stone, brittle vegetation, and reflective accents.
- Grazelands and highlands: open sightlines, wind-shaped grasses, camp markers, and long route silhouettes.
- Caves: stronger contrast, simpler color groups, readable exits, and creature/ore/ritual tells.

## Anti-Goals

- No generic Minecraft-like hotbar/build visual language.
- No single-hue biome wash.
- No decorative props that do not help navigation, story, ecology, or interaction.
- No giant UI cards over the world.
- No unreadable terrain noise where the player needs to travel.

## Review Standard

Judge visual changes from a player screenshot and a short walk, not from isolated implementation intent. A change is good when the region is recognizable, the route is readable, important silhouettes survive fog and distance, and the frame still feels like the same authored island.
