# Gameplay Exploration Plan

## Loop

The first playable loop is exploration, not combat or building:

1. See a landmark, route sign, weather cue, cave mouth, creature sign, or vegetation cluster.
2. Move toward it using readable world language.
3. Inspect, read, use, collect, observe, listen, or enter.
4. Record progress in journals, skills, route goals, bestiary signs, loot, and quest hooks.
5. Let the new record change the next available prompt or navigation target.

## Interaction Verbs

- `inspect`: study landmarks, creature signs, route objects, vegetation, ruins, and encounter traces.
- `read`: decode shrines, road marks, warnings, and travel notes.
- `use`: perform a clear local action such as marking a route, checking a pack, entering a cave, or advancing sky-watch time.
- `collect`: gather coherent loot or forage discovered from the area, landmarks, or mob signs.
- `listen`: record ambient soundscape clues.

## Systems To Grow

- Exploration event log as the source for recent discoveries and repeatable observations.
- Discovery journal for biomes, landmarks, caves, and notable world features.
- Skill journal for Naturalist, Lore, Pathfinding, Survival, and related unlocks.
- Travel goals that complete through player movement and interaction.
- Passive mob signs that point toward ecology, forage, danger, or region identity.
- Loot and forage state that reacts to previous observations.
- Cave traversal that preserves a sense of place and return.

## Acceptance

A slice is worth keeping when a player can discover something new, understand why it belongs where it is, and see a concrete state change without reading debug output.
